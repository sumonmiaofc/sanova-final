Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your visit. Still have questions? Call (520) 232-2525.

Do I need an appointment? ⌄

No appointment needed. Sanova is a walk-in clinic — stop by Mon–Sat, 7AM–7PM. Most patients are seen in under 30 minutes.

Urgent care vs ER — what's the difference? ⌄

Urgent care treats non-life-threatening conditions like sprains, infections, and fevers. The ER handles emergencies like chest pain or major trauma. Urgent care is faster and costs a fraction of the ER.

How much without insurance? ⌄

We offer transparent self-pay rates significantly lower than ER costs. Call (520) 232-2525 for current pricing.

What insurance do you accept? ⌄

Medicare, Medicaid, Tricare, UnitedHealthcare, Optum, BCBS, Aetna, Cigna, Arizona Complete Health, and more. Call us to confirm your plan.

What should I bring? ⌄

Photo ID, insurance card, list of medications, and relevant medical history. For minors, a parent or guardian must be present.

Do you treat children? ⌄

Yes. Our board-certified providers treat patients of all ages — from young children to seniors.

X-rays and lab work on-site? ⌄

Yes — digital X-ray and rapid lab testing for flu, strep, COVID-19, UTIs, STDs, and blood panels. Results before you leave.

What conditions do you treat? ⌄

Colds, flu, fever, allergies, UTIs, sprains, fractures, lacerations, burns, and more. Also sports, school, and DOT physicals.

How long is the wait? ⌄

Most patients are seen in under 30 minutes. Busiest times are lunchtime, early evening, and Mondays.

Where are you located? ⌄

Serving Saddlebrooke, Catalina, Rancho Vistoso, Oro Valley, Sun City, Oracle, Mammoth, San Manuel — North Tucson, with on-site parking.

COVID-19 testing available? ⌄

Yes — rapid COVID-19, flu, and strep testing on-site. Results available before you leave.

Are you open Sundays? ⌄

Closed Sundays. Open Monday–Saturday, 7AM–7PM. No appointment needed.
